# Executive Summary

## Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the most critical challenges faced by subscription-based businesses. This project analyzes customer behavior, identifies key factors contributing to churn, and develops a machine learning model capable of predicting customers who are likely to leave.

The project combines SQL, Python, Machine Learning, and Power BI to deliver an end-to-end customer churn analytics solution.

---

## Objectives

- Understand customer demographics and service usage.
- Identify the primary drivers of customer churn.
- Build predictive machine learning models.
- Compare multiple classification algorithms.
- Visualize business insights through an interactive Power BI dashboard.
- Recommend data-driven customer retention strategies.

---

## Methodology

The project followed a structured analytics workflow:

1. Data Cleaning & Preprocessing
2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
3. Feature Engineering
4. Machine Learning Model Development
5. Model Evaluation
6. Business Insight Generation
7. Interactive Dashboard Development

---

## Key Findings

- Month-to-Month contract customers have the highest churn rate.
- Fiber Optic users are more likely to churn compared to DSL customers.
- Customers using Electronic Check exhibit higher churn.
- Short-tenure customers are at greater risk of leaving.
- High monthly charges significantly contribute to churn.
- Customer service experience plays an important role in customer retention.

---

## Best Performing Model

After evaluating multiple machine learning algorithms, **Logistic Regression** achieved the best overall balance between performance and interpretability.

**Model Accuracy:** **80.34%**
